Send DUST-20 safely

When you send DUST-20, the rest needs a home.

If you send part of a larger DUST-20 amount, the amount you keep needs its own DUST-20 change output. The Bitcoin fee must be paid with separate regular bitcoin.

The simple ruleExampleSpecial casesBitcoin feeOpen questions

The simple rule

Do not use DUST-20 backing to pay the Bitcoin fee.

A safe send keeps three things separate: what the receiver gets, what you keep as DUST-20 change, and the regular bitcoin that pays the network fee.

starting DUST-20 backing = receiver backing + your DUST-20 changeThe Bitcoin network fee belongs outside this equation. The legacy guide does not define a burn model.

Example

Send 10 units, keep 81 units as change.

This documented example uses 546 sats for each unit. The point is simple: your remaining units need a visible place to go.

You start with49,686 sats91 DUST-20 units at 546 sats each
→
Receiver gets5,460 sats10 DUST-20 units
You keep as DUST-20 change44,226 sats81 DUST-20 units
1 / PICK

Choose the DUST-20 you mean to send

Keep the DUST-20 output separate from the regular bitcoin used for fees. A good app shows what it selected and why.

2 / CALCULATE

Work in whole units and sats

Calculate the receiver amount and your change with exact whole numbers. Each DUST-20 output must follow its sats-per-unit rule.

3 / REVIEW

Give every output a clear job

Show the receiver amount, your DUST-20 change, regular bitcoin change, and fee funding separately. A list of addresses is not enough.

Special cases

Keep it simple when the rules are not fully settled.

The legacy guide does not define every possible merge or whole-output move. A cautious app only combines DUST-20 amounts when it can show that they use the same accepted rule.

COMBINE

Only combine matching DUST-20

Combine inputs only when the app can show that they use the same ticker and sats-per-unit rule. The new backing must equal the exact total.

SEND EVERYTHING

No change when nothing remains

If you send every selected DUST-20 sat to the receiver, you do not need DUST-20 change. The review should still say clearly that everything is being sent.

Bitcoin fee

Pay the miner with regular bitcoin.

The network fee is regular Bitcoin value. It must not be quietly taken from the sats that back your DUST-20.

SAFE

Use separate fee funding

Add enough regular bitcoin to pay the fee. The DUST-20 amount and DUST-20 change stay exactly balanced.

UNSAFE

Do not shave the fee from DUST-20

If a 49,686-sat DUST-20 input creates only 49,000 sats of DUST-20 outputs, 686 sats have no clear role. Do not treat that as a safe DUST-20 send just because Bitcoin confirms it.

!

Missing DUST-20 change is a stop sign

This is not a small formatting issue. The DUST-20 you meant to keep may become hard to track or may be read differently by another wallet or balance service. Do not approve it.

Open questions

Some details are not settled yet.

The legacy material does not define every transfer rule. Until tools publish a shared policy, do not guess when the result is ambiguous.

QuestionWhy it mattersSafe current posture
Is there a canonical transfer inscription?The legacy guide documents deploy and mint JSON, not a full transfer schema.Label application transfer payloads implementation-specific.
How are multiple colored inputs mapped to outputs?Without a deterministic mapping, a merge or split can have competing readings.Reject ambiguous or mixed-token selections.
Does output order matter?Ordinal and inscription assignment can be sensitive to output ordering.Document and test the exact builder behavior.
What is an intentional burn or unlock?There is no legacy burn definition.Show recovery actions as potentially allocation-destroying, not canonical burns.
What happens after RBF or a reorg?A confirmed-looking state can be replaced or rolled back.Track confirmation and invalidate derived state on chain changes.
